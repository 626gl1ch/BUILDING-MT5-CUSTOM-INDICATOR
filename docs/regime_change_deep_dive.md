# Deep Dive: Detecting Regime Change in Computational Finance

*Based on the concepts from "Detecting Regime Change in Computational Finance" by Jun Chen and Edward P. K. Tsang.*

## Core Philosophy: Event-Based vs. Time-Based Sampling
Traditional algorithmic trading samples data at fixed time intervals (e.g., 5-minute bars, daily closes). This arbitrary slicing often misses the true heartbeat of the market, cutting trends in half or aggregating volatile swings into meaningless "Doji" candles. 

The book introduces the **Directional Change (DC)** framework. DC is an *event-based* sampling method. Instead of recording price every 5 minutes, it only records a data point when the price changes direction by a specific threshold (e.g., a 1% zigzag). 

This strips away the "noise" of time and focuses purely on intrinsic market volatility.

---

## 1. The Directional Change (DC) Algorithm

A DC event occurs when the price drops or rises from an extreme point by a predefined threshold ($\theta$).

### The Anatomy of a DC Trend
When analyzing a market via DC, every price movement is broken into two phases:
1. **Directional Change (DC) Event:** The initial price move that confirms a trend reversal (e.g., a 1% drop from the absolute peak).
2. **Overshoot (OS) Event:** The continuation of that trend *after* the DC event is confirmed, until the next reversal occurs.

### Why is this powerful?
By measuring the length and duration of the OS (Overshoot) events across different thresholds ($\theta$), you can quantify the exact "momentum" of the market. If a market constantly has massive Overshoots, it is a trending regime. If the Overshoots are tiny and it immediately reverses (triggering a new DC event), it is a mean-reverting or choppy regime.

## 2. Hidden Markov Models (HMM) for Regime Detection

A Hidden Markov Model (HMM) is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm. It assumes that the observable data (the price) is generated by a "hidden" state (the market regime).

### How it integrates with DC
Instead of feeding raw prices into the HMM, the authors feed **DC-based indicators** into the HMM. 

1. **Calculate DC Indicators:**
   - **Time for DC ($T_{dc}$):** How long did it take for the trend to reverse?
   - **Time for OS ($T_{os}$):** How long did the trend continue after the reversal was confirmed?
   - **Total Price Movement (TM):** The total distance from the peak to the trough.

2. **Train the HMM:**
   - You feed the historical stream of $T_{dc}$, $T_{os}$, and TM into a 2-state or 3-state HMM.
   - The HMM clusters the data without supervision, mathematically determining when the market shifts from "State 0" (e.g., Low Volatility/Trending) to "State 1" (e.g., High Volatility/Mean Reverting).

### The Math (Simplified)
The HMM calculates the **Transition Probability Matrix**. It tells you:
* "If we are in a Trending Regime today, there is a 92% chance we stay in a Trending Regime tomorrow, and an 8% chance we shift to a Choppy Regime."

## 3. Practical Implementation for Algorithmic Trading

To build a trading strategy using this framework in your bot:

### Step 1: The Regime Filter
We build a Python script using the `hmmlearn` library. 
1. We transform the 5-min OHLCV data into a stream of DC events (e.g., 0.5% threshold).
2. We train a Gaussian HMM on a rolling window of the last 30 days.
3. Every hour, the HMM outputs a prediction: `Regime 0` or `Regime 1`.

### Step 2: Adaptive Strategy Allocation
Instead of turning strategies on and off blindly, we map them to the HMM output:
* **If HMM detects Regime 0 (Trending):** The Meta-Controller allocates 100% of capital to the `ema_pullback` and `donchian_breakout` strategies.
* **If HMM detects Regime 1 (Mean Reverting):** The Meta-Controller scales trend strategies down to 0%, and allocates maximum leverage to the `bb_vwap_mr` (Mean Reversion) strategy.

### Step 3: DC-Based Stop Losses
Because DC measures the average "Overshoot", you can set your Stop Loss exactly at the $95^{th}$ percentile of historical Overshoots for that specific regime. If the price moves past that point, you mathematically know the regime has broken.
